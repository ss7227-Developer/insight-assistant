Human Resources Policy Manual — Sample Excerpt
===============================================

Section 4: Leave and Time-Off Policies
---------------------------------------

4.1 Annual Leave
Employees are entitled to 20 days of paid annual leave per calendar year,
accrued at 1.67 days per month. Unused leave may be carried forward up to
a maximum of 10 days into the following year. Leave must be approved by
the direct manager at least 5 business days in advance for periods of 5
or more consecutive days.

4.2 Sick Leave
Employees are entitled to 10 days of paid sick leave per year. A medical
certificate is required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days. Unused
sick leave does not carry forward and is not paid out on termination.

4.3 Parental Leave
Primary caregivers are entitled to 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave.
Secondary caregivers are entitled to 4 weeks of fully paid parental leave.
Parental leave must be taken within 12 months of the child's birth or
adoption placement.

Section 5: Performance Management
----------------------------------

5.1 Review Cycle
Performance reviews are conducted twice per year: mid-year (June) and
year-end (December). All employees are assessed on a 5-point scale across
four dimensions: technical proficiency, collaboration, communication, and
strategic impact.

5.2 Compensation Adjustments
Merit increases are awarded annually in January, based on the year-end
performance rating. Employees rated 4 or 5 are eligible for increases
of 5-10%. Employees rated 3 receive a standard cost-of-living adjustment
of 2-3%. Employees rated below 3 are placed on a Performance Improvement
Plan (PIP).

5.3 Performance Improvement Plans
A PIP is a 90-day structured program with clearly defined goals, weekly
check-ins, and measurable success criteria. Failure to meet PIP objectives
may result in termination of employment.

Section 6: Code of Conduct
---------------------------

6.1 Confidentiality
All employees must maintain strict confidentiality of client data, proprietary
algorithms, and business strategies. Disclosure of confidential information
to unauthorized parties is grounds for immediate termination.

6.2 Conflicts of Interest
Employees must disclose any potential conflicts of interest to HR and their
manager. This includes board memberships, investments in competitors, or
family members working at client organizations.

6.3 Workplace Respect
Acme Analytics Corp is committed to a workplace free from harassment,
discrimination, and bullying. All complaints are investigated within 10
business days by the HR department.
